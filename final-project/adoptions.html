Starter Guide For Getting a Dog

Where to Start

You’ve decided it is time to adopt a new dog, so now what? There are many things to consider before bringing home your four-legged friend. First, you’ll need to make sure you are sure. Having a dogs means years of commitment. The average lifespan of a dog is 10-13 years, so while it might seem crazy to look that far into the future, prepare yourself for your dog’s company for that long. Once you decided if adopting a new pet is feasible, you’ll have to start the dog search. Knowing what breed, size, age and characteristics you prefer helps narrow down your options. After you have a good idea of what you want in your pup, you’ll have to decide between going through a breeder or shelter. While a breeder is a lot easier to find exactly what you are looking for, adoption is a great way to rescue a furry friend. For either option, it is important to look into where your dog is coming from.

What to Look For in Breeders

If you make the decision to get your dog from a breeder, there are several things you’ll need to consider. Breeders are essentially small business owners, so it is possible that they might charge a lot of money for a dog that might not have a lot of training. Looking into the living conditions of the breeder can help determine whether or not the dogs are receiving appropriate care. A good quality breeder cares a lot about where their dogs are going. Your relationship with your dog’s breeder should help ensure that the transition is smooth.

What to Look For in Shelters

Adopting a dog from a shelter is usually a more affordable and rewarding option. However, when rescuing a dog from a shelter, you most likely will not know a lot about the dog’s past. A good shelter will provide you with as much information on the dog as possible, to ensure that the dog will make a great addition to your family. It is recommended that you ask the shelter how the dog has been behaving under their surveillance. If a shelter is not paying much attention to the dog, then it might be best to look into other shelters.

Preparing for Your Puppy

Now that you have decided on the pup of your dreams, you’ll have to start preparing. Designating a certain part of the house for your dog to eat, sleep and play helps keep things more organized. Additionally, blocking off areas that are “off limits” will help keep your furry friend out of trouble. Baby gates are a great way to puppy-proof your home. You’ll also need to get the essentials. Having food, water, leash and collars, and a dog bed are just some of the things you’ll need before bringing the dog home. Lastly, you have to do your research to find a good veterinarian in your area. Finding the right vet might seem challenging but asking neighbors and friends in your community is a great way to find the perfect fit!
